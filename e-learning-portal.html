Athlon E-Learning Portal

Overview

As Athlon Studio undergoes rapid expansion, a crucial requirement emerged for a centralized learning hub to enhance onboarding and provide continuous learning resources. This rapid 2-week project aimed to create an accessible platform catering to employees' UX design education needs.Â 

This initiative addresses the company's growth demands, accentuating learning efficiency and exemplifying Athlon Studio's commitment to fostering a culture of ongoing personal and team development. The e-learning portal empowers employees to access UX/UI design resources at their convenience, contributing to a dynamic learning environment.

Problem

1. Athlon Studio's rapid growth makes it challenging to share knowledge and processes across the company, its employees and various communication channels.

2. New designers joining the team encounter challenges during onboarding as they are unfamiliar with Athlon Studio's design procedures.

3. There is a lack of centralized design standards or comprehensive documentation to set clear expectations for design work and skills at Athlon Studio.

Research

Internal Site Survey

One of the key findings from the internal survey was how employees learned about new tools within the organization. The results of this question shed light on a distinct gap between learning through formal onboarding and learning through discussions. While 50% of participants reported relying on company onboarding, a significant 95.5% emphasized the influential role of colleague-driven discussions in discovering new tools. This striking contrast highlights the need for enhanced onboarding efforts to bridge this gap.

Design Operations Card-Sorting

This initiative emerged as a pivotal research effort driven by one-on-one interviews with numerous employees. This exercise involved sorting lessons and identifying potential categories.

Some employee feedback received, including quotes such as "mini courses with different modules" and "...I need to know how to do this specific task," highlighted the demand for a centralized learning environment.

Solutions

1. By prioritizing simplicity and user-friendly design, we've set to create a platform that accelerates the learning curve, enabling new hires to quickly access crucial design knowledge and internal procedures.

2. A focused approach on a minimum viable product ensures that the platform meets its primary objective efficiently, addressing current hiring needs while establishing a platform that can be developed with ease.

3. The user-centric design guarantees a smooth navigation experience and an enjoyable learning path, effectively addressing the challenge of complex and cumbersome internal learning systems, ultimately enhancing productivity.

Ideation

1. Implementing a single-page scroll for lessons to ensure a smooth and uninterrupted learning journey.

2. Incorporating three tabs for structured learning tailored to different levels.

3. Featuring a dedicated section for director's insights, fostering a connection between leadership and employees.

Concepts

In the pursuit of effective design, three low-fidelity digital wireframes were crafted, each tackling critical challenges. The focus centered around finding optimal ways to present lessons, navigating through an extensive array of content, and ensuring digestibility of information. The following highlights encapsulate the key takeaways and evolution of these concepts:

Concept 1

Featuring a single-page layout, showcasing lessons with thumbnail previews, titles, and brief descriptions. The side navigation provides access to the three main learning categories, which, when expanded, reveals their corresponding subcategories.

Concept 2

Incorporating a top navigation for the three main categories, while the side navigation is static displaying categories and their lessons. The main content section showcases lesson details, including title, author, image, description, and a button for lesson access.

Concept 3

Introducing an on-page tab selector to choose from the three main categories. The main content area showcases courses through video thumbnails, course titles, and descriptions.

Variations

Through an iterative process guided by client feedback, a cohesive design was crafted by amalgamating elements from Concept 1 and Concept 2. This hybrid approach was tailored to best address Athlon Studio's specific learning needs.

Variation 1

Adopting a top navigation bar for effortless selection of learning categories. The left side navigation complements this, offering swift access to subcategories within each course category.

Variation 2

Variation 2 draws attention with pill-shaped, vibrant green lesson categories that captivate the user's focus. There's no top navigation; instead, the left navigation contains the main categories. Upon selection, users are directed to the selected category page, which then expands to showcase all lesson subcategories.

Variation 3

This variation utilizes category separation through typeface hierarchy. The left navigation houses the main categories and their subsequent lessons.

Final Designs

Through an iterative process guided by user feedback and client preferences, the selected design seamlessly integrates efficient navigation, engaging content presentation, and user-centric functionality. The culmination of research-driven insights and design innovation ensures the creation of a tailored e-learning platform that addresses Athlon's growth needs and empowers continuous learning within the organization.
